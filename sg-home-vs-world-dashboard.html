Method, data, and what is still not modelled
  • Buyer and renter start with identical cash and identical annual housing budgets; whoever spends less invests the difference in the benchmark. Bank loans reprice annually on the historical rate series (approximate SIBOR/SORA-era averages).
  • HDB RPI is official; URA private 2004–2025 verified, 1994–2003 reconstructed. MSCI World gross USD converted at year-end FX; drag inputs convert gross to a realistic net for an Irish-UCITS investor (e.g. IWDA/VWRA).
  • Modelled: CPF accrued-interest refund at 2.5% on the CPF-funded share (locked liquidity, not lost wealth), HDB lease-decay drag (default 0.5%/yr vs the index), behavioural-gap control (baseline: fully disciplined renter at 100%), real-terms view via SG CPI.
  • Still excluded, favouring the renter-investor: ABSD on second properties, SSD inside 3 years, single-flat dispersion beyond the decay input. Still excluded, favouring the buyer: the rent-inflation hedge of fixed housing costs.
  • Grants and BTO discounts vary by income, flat type and era; defaults are mid-range.

Data & caveats for this tab
  • Income: official MOM median gross monthly income of full-time employed residents, incl. employer CPF (1996–2025). 1995 extrapolated; 2000 and 2005 interpolated (survey not run those years). Includes employer CPF, which is appropriate here since CPF services housing.
  • Household basis multiplies individual income by a ratio drifting from ~1.65× (1995) to ~2.05× (2025), reflecting the rise of dual-income households — note this coping mechanism is itself part of the affordability story.
  • Prices are index-scaled from your 2025 anchor prices, so levels inherit the quality-adjusted index; actual median transacted prices also shifted with flat sizes and mix.
  • Real-wages framing: deflating both wages and prices by CPI cancels out in any ratio — price-to-income and servicing ratios are already the real comparison.
  • Median full-time income excludes part-timers and the unemployed; affordability for those groups is worse than shown.

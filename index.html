The textbook answer breaks here.

Four facts make Singapore's rent-vs-buy math unlike anywhere else.

~78%
live in HDB flats
Sold below market, with grants. The first buyer's return starts with a state transfer.
0%
tax on gains
Homes and ETFs alike. The tax-shelter case for owning doesn't exist here.
2.5%
the CPF hurdle
Housing money's alternative is CPF OA, not equities at ~8%. That collapses the renter's edge.
Capped
both tails
Cooling measures cap booms, supply control cushions busts. A policy collar on prices.

The playbook the numbers point to.

Three moves, in order.

1
Take the subsidised first homeThe BTO discount and grant are a one-time transfer you can't get any other way. They explain most of the buyer's edge in the decomposition on tab one.
2
Finance it with cheap, capped leverageAn HDB loan at 2.6% against CPF money earning 2.5% is nearly free carry, and the mortgage doubles as forced saving.
3
Send every further dollar to global marketsABSD makes a second property punitive, and unlevered SG property lags world equities badly. Once the flat is secured, the marginal dollar belongs in the index.

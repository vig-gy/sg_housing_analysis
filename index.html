    In one screen · why the textbook rent-vs-buy answer breaks here
    The state is the developer
    ~78% live in HDB flats sold below market with grants. The first buyer's "return" starts with a fiscal transfer, not a market gain.
    Nobody's gains are taxed
    No capital gains tax on homes or ETFs. The Canadian tax-shelter argument for owning simply doesn't exist here.
    CPF changes the hurdle
    The median buyer's alternative for housing money is CPF OA at 2.5%, not equities at ~8%. That collapses the renter's advantage.
    Policy caps both tails
    Cooling measures cap booms; supply control cushions busts. Property behaves like an inflation-linked bond with a policy collar.

    Expert note: what this does to expected returns

    Suppressed volatility plus capped upside means Singapore residential property is best modelled not as an equity-like asset but as a leveraged, inflation-tracking bond with an embedded policy put and call. Its unlevered Sharpe ratio is respectable; its unlevered return is not. Everything interesting in the first tab comes from the financing and subsidy structure around it, not the asset itself — HDB prices compounded ~3.4% and private ~2.2% over 1995–2025 versus ~7–8% for global equities in SGD.

    Two constraints, two charts · how to read the affordability tab
    S
    Stock constraint — years-of-income chartHow long to accumulate the downpayment. Driven by price-to-income. This is what people mean when they say "housing is unaffordable."
    F
    Flow constraint — servicing-ratio chartCan this month's income carry this month's instalment at this year's interest rate. This is what MAS actually regulates (30% MSR), and it moves opposite to prices when rates swing: 2021's record prices were cheaper to carry than 1997's.
    Expert note: the three traps the affordability tab is built to avoid

    1) Deflator cancellation: real price ÷ real wage = nominal price ÷ nominal wage; CPI divides out. Ratio charts are already the real comparison. 2) Base-year gambling: any index-vs-index chart can be tuned to a narrative by picking the base; the selector exposes this instead of hiding it. 3) Unit mismatch: individuals earn wages, households buy flats; the household multiplier rose ~1.65×→2.05× as dual incomes became the norm — which flatters affordability while embedding the coping mechanism in the measure. Both bases are shown for that reason.

    Expert note: the finding most people don't expect

    Over the full 30 years, median incomes (~3.9%/yr nominal) outgrew both HDB (~3.4%) and private (~2.2%) price indices. The squeeze is real but episodic — 1993–96, 2007–13, 2020–24 — and the conclusion only holds for continuously employed full-time residents, from a 1995 base that sits mid-boom. Shift the base to 1990 or drop to part-time workers and it weakens. Precision about the claim is the difference between analysis and narrative.

    The rational Singapore playbook that falls out of the numbers
    1
    Take the subsidised first propertyThe BTO discount and grant are a one-time state transfer you cannot get any other way; era-scaled, they explain most of the buyer's edge in the decomposition below the bar chart on tab one.
    2
    Finance it with cheap, capped leverageThe HDB loan at 2.6% against CPF money earning 2.5% is nearly free carry. The mortgage doubles as forced saving — the behavioural gap toggle shows how much that discipline is worth.
    3
    Send every further dollar to global marketsABSD makes a second property punitively expensive, and unlevered SG property lags world equities badly. The video's "home as diversifier" point lands here: once the flat is secured, the marginal dollar belongs in the index.
    Simple summary (for a beginner audience)

    In most countries, rent-and-invest vs buy is a close call. In Singapore, the government pays you to buy your first home and taxes you heavily for buying a second. So: take the subsidised flat, use the cheap loan, and invest everything else in a low-cost world ETF. Don't treat property as your growth engine — treat it as your stability, and let the world index do the compounding.
